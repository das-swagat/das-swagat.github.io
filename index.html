About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Virginia Commonwealth University working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and cyber-physical systems. My research focuses on developing scalable, data-driven learning methods that bridge theoretical machine learning with real-world deployment in critical infrastructure, sensing, and intelligent systems.

I am currently a Graduate Research Assistant with the Modern Heuristics Research Group (MHRG). My work spans self-supervised and representation learning for anomaly detection, AI-enabled security for cyber-physical systems, and 3D LiDAR point-cloud segmentation for large-scale urban and environmental datasets.
