About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Virginia Commonwealth University working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, deep learning, and cybersecurity. My work focuses on building data-driven systems that operate reliably outside controlled environments, particularly in cyber-physical systems and large-scale infrastructure settings where constraints, uncertainty, and robustness are critical.

My research spans self-supervised learning for anomaly detection, scalable machine learning pipelines, and 3D LiDAR perception. I work on problems where labeled data is limited, systems evolve over time, and deployment requirements are as important as model performance.

Before starting my PhD, I served as an Assistant Professor with over 5000 hours of university teaching experience. My work included curriculum development, accreditation processes (ABET, NBA, QS), industry collaborations, and mentoring international students across diverse academic programs.
